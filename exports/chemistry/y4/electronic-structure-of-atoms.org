<div id="outline-container-org9b60299" class="outline-2">
<h2 id="org9b60299"><span class="section-number-2">1</span> Theory</h2>
<div class="outline-text-2" id="text-1">
</div>
<div id="outline-container-orgb6f7962" class="outline-3">
<h3 id="orgb6f7962"><span class="section-number-3">1.1</span> Principal quantum number, n</h3>
<div class="outline-text-3" id="text-1-1">
<p>
Relates to the average relative distance of the electron from the nucleus or energy level
</p>

<p>
Larger n -&gt; larger orbital &amp; higher energy level (closer to zero)
</p>

<p>
A collection of oribtals with same n is called a principal/electron shell
</p>
</div>
</div>

<div id="outline-container-org61672c2" class="outline-3">
<h3 id="org61672c2"><span class="section-number-3">1.2</span> Angular momentum quantum number, l</h3>
<div class="outline-text-3" id="text-1-2">
<p>
l = 0,1,2,&#x2026;,n-1
(i.e. spdf)
</p>

<p>
Describes the shape of the volume of space occupied by the electron
</p>

<p>
Defines subshell
</p>
</div>
</div>

<div id="outline-container-org5cf1675" class="outline-3">
<h3 id="org5cf1675"><span class="section-number-3">1.3</span> Magnetic quantum number, m<sub>l</sub></h3>
<div class="outline-text-3" id="text-1-3">
<p>
-l to l
</p>

<p>
number of m<sub>l</sub> values indicate number of orbitals. It describes the 3-D orientation of the orbital occupied by the electron wrt an applied magnetic field.
</p>

<p>
e.g. 2p<sub>x</sub>, 2p<sub>y</sub>, 2p<sub>z</sub>
e.g. [3d] yz, xz, xy , x<sup>2</sup>-y<sup>2</sup>, z<sup>2</sup>
</p>

<p>
same m<sub>l</sub> -&gt; same energy (degenerate)
</p>
</div>
</div>

<div id="outline-container-orge02bdbb" class="outline-3">
<h3 id="orge02bdbb"><span class="section-number-3">1.4</span> Electron spin quantum number, m<sub>s</sub></h3>
<div class="outline-text-3" id="text-1-4">
<p>
accounts for possible orientations of the electron when magnetic field is applied
</p>
</div>
</div>

<div id="outline-container-org3bfbc74" class="outline-3">
<h3 id="org3bfbc74"><span class="section-number-3">1.5</span> Magnetism</h3>
<div class="outline-text-3" id="text-1-5">
</div>
<div id="outline-container-org773aeeb" class="outline-4">
<h4 id="org773aeeb"><span class="section-number-4">1.5.1</span> Paramagnetism</h4>
<div class="outline-text-4" id="text-1-5-1">
<p>
unpaired electrons, attracted by magnets
There's net spin in at least one of its atomic orbitals
</p>
</div>
</div>

<div id="outline-container-org3767278" class="outline-4">
<h4 id="org3767278"><span class="section-number-4">1.5.2</span> Diamagnetism</h4>
<div class="outline-text-4" id="text-1-5-2">
<p>
Slightly repelled by magnets
There is no net spin in its atomic orbitals
</p>
</div>
</div>
</div>

<div id="outline-container-org019118b" class="outline-3">
<h3 id="org019118b"><span class="section-number-3">1.6</span> Anomalous electron config</h3>
<div class="outline-text-3" id="text-1-6">
<p>
Cr = [Ar] \(3d^54s^1\)
Cu = [Ar] \(3d^{10}4s^1\)
</p>

<p>
Due to added stability associated with half-filled and completely-filled subshells
</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="outline-text-3" id="text-1-7">
<ul class="org-ul">
<li>Hund rule: minimise spin pairs</li>
<li>aufbau rule: zig zag</li>
</ul>
